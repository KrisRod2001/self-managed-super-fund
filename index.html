Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSF) and Property Conveyancing

Expert guidance on buying and selling property through your SMSF in Australia

What is an SMSF?

Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSFs) have become increasingly popular in Australia, especially since legislative changes in 2007 allowed SMSFs to borrow for investment properties via Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangements (LRBAs). An SMSF gives you control over your retirement savings, including the ability to invest directly in property for potential capital growth and rental income.

Benefits of Investing in Property Through an SMSF

  • Tax advantages: Rental income taxed at 15%, potential capital gains tax discounts (up to 10% effective rate if held over 12 months).
  • Diversification of your super portfolio with real estate.
  • Control over investment decisions.
  • Ability to borrow under strict LRBA rules for higher-value properties.
  • Asset protection and potential for retirement income stream.

Key Rules and Restrictions

All SMSF investments must pass the sole purpose test: solely for providing retirement benefits.

  • Cannot purchase residential property from related parties.
  • No personal use: Fund members or relatives cannot live in or use the property.
  • Commercial property can be leased to related parties at market rates.
  • Borrowing only via LRBA, with the property held in a separate bare trust.
  • Must be a single acquirable asset (generally one property per loan).
  • Strict compliance required to avoid severe penalties from the ATO.

The Conveyancing Process for SMSF Property

SMSF property transactions are more complex than standard conveyancing due to superannuation laws. Key steps include:

  • Contract review to ensure correct entity (SMSF trustee or bare trust).
  • Compliance checks with ATO rules and investment strategy.
  • Coordination of finance (if borrowing via LRBA).
  • Stamp duty assessment and payment.
  • Settlement and title registration in the correct name.
  • Post-settlement transfer from bare trust to SMSF once loan is repaid.
